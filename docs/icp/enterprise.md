# ICP — Enterprise

## The mode
They are governing a shipping operation that spans multiple regions, multiple ERPs, and carrier contracts negotiated in three different time zones. Today they are preparing a quarterly carrier performance review and realising, again, that the data they need lives in four different systems. They are not looking for a new tool. They are looking for a reason to consolidate what they already have.

## Who they are
- Job title(s): VP of Logistics, Group Supply Chain Director, Head of Global Operations, Director of Fulfilment, Chief Operating Officer
- Company size: £50m+ revenue, 5,000+ parcels per day
- Team size: 10–40 person ops function across multiple sites or regions
- Tooling: SAP or Oracle NetSuite as ERP, a WMS (Manhattan, Blue Yonder, or custom-built), 6–12 carrier portals, often a legacy TMS
- Reporting line: Reports to COO or CFO; direct reports include regional logistics managers and a dispatch supervisor team

## Top 3 pains (in their words)
1. **"We have 40 carrier relationships and no single view of what any of them cost us"** — Negotiated rate cards sit in email threads and carrier portals. Reconciling actual spend against contracted rates takes the finance team two weeks every quarter and still produces numbers nobody trusts.
2. **"Every new market we enter becomes a 6-month IT project just to get shipping working"** — Adding a carrier or a region means a new API engagement, a new label format, a new data field in the ERP. The business moves faster than IT can keep up, and shipments go out manually in the gap.
3. **"Our ERP says one thing and our WMS says another and the carrier portal says a third"** — Shipment data lives in silos. The reconciliation overhead before every carrier SLA review eats two days of analyst time and the numbers are still disputed.

## Top 3 jobs-to-be-done
1. **When I am entering a new market, I want carrier connectivity to be live within days, so I can launch without an IT project holding the business back.**
2. **When I am reviewing carrier performance across regions, I want a single data set covering all lanes and all carriers, so I can negotiate from a position of fact rather than estimate.**
3. **When my ERP confirms an order, I want carrier selection and booking to happen automatically against pre-set routing rules, so my dispatch team focuses on exceptions rather than routine decisions.**

## Conversion triggers (what makes them book a call)
1. A carrier they rely on announces a rate increase and they realise they cannot quickly benchmark it against alternatives at scale.
2. A failed audit or customs hold in a new market exposes the gap between their compliance process and what the destination country requires.
3. A board-level directive to reduce logistics cost by a fixed percentage arrives and they have no data infrastructure to know where to start.

## Disqualifiers (what makes them bounce)
1. The product looks like it is built for SMBs. No mention of SAP or NetSuite integration, no multi-region capability, no dedicated account management.
2. They cannot get a clear answer on how the API connects to their specific ERP version within the first conversation.
3. The onboarding timeline is measured in months, not weeks. Their IT steering committee will not approve a 9-month integration project.
4. There is no named account manager. Ticket-based support is not acceptable at their volume.

## Competitive context
Enterprise buyers typically run a fragmented stack: a legacy TMS for freight, carrier portals for parcels, and manual processes bridging the two. When they evaluate change, they look at Metapack and Descartes for enterprise-grade multi-carrier management, and at their existing ERP vendor's native shipping modules (SAP TM, NetSuite WMS) as a zero-integration alternative. The real competition is inertia: the current system is painful but known, and IT resource is always scarce.

## Primary conversion goal
Book a 40-minute platform review with a senior account executive, scoped to their specific ERP environment and carrier footprint.

## Voice notes for this ICP
- Lead with consolidation metrics and data accuracy before any feature. They have heard about features. They want to know what the data looks like after six months.
- Name their ERP. "Works with SAP" lands harder than "integrates with your existing systems."
- Quantified ROI framing: time saved in reconciliation, cost saved in carrier spend, headcount avoided in new-market launches.
- Do not use the word "simple." Their operation is not simple and they know it. Say "predictable" instead.
- The SwiftLog case study (client onboarding from 2 weeks to 2 days, 98.7% accuracy) translates well here as a proxy for operational control at scale.
