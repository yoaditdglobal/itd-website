# ICP — Small Business

## The mode
They are packing and dispatching 20 to 150 orders a day, usually from a spare room, a small unit, or a shared warehouse. They are the ops team. They print labels, they handle returns calls, they check tracking when a customer emails. They are not looking for a platform. They are looking for two hours back each day and a lower per-parcel cost.

## Who they are
- Job title(s): Founder, Owner-Operator, Operations Manager, Fulfilment Manager, eCommerce Manager
- Company size: £100k–£2m revenue, 20–200 parcels per day
- Team size: 1–4 people touching shipping
- Tooling: Shopify or WooCommerce for orders, Royal Mail Click and Drop or Evri direct portal for labels, spreadsheet for tracking
- Reporting line: Reports to nobody or directly to the business owner; often is the business owner

## Top 3 pains (in their words)
1. **"I'm spending two hours every morning just printing labels"** — Each order goes through the carrier website one at a time: paste the address, choose the service, print the label. At 60 orders a day, that is the first two hours of every working morning gone before the real work starts.
2. **"I know I'm not on the best rates but I don't have the volume to negotiate"** — Royal Mail rack rates are expensive. Evri is cheaper but not always right. There is no easy way to compare on each individual shipment, so you pick one carrier and stick with it even when it costs more than it should.
3. **"When a customer asks where their parcel is, I have to log into the carrier website to find out"** — WISMO queries arrive via email, social DM, and phone. Answering each one means a carrier portal login, a tracking number search, and a copy-paste back to the customer. At 10 queries a day that is 30 minutes of context-switching.

## Top 3 jobs-to-be-done
1. **When I have 60 orders to dispatch, I want labels generated in one batch without logging into multiple carrier portals, so I can be done before 10am and focus on the business.**
2. **When I am booking a shipment, I want to see the actual cost across Royal Mail, Evri, and DPD side by side, so I stop defaulting to the most familiar option and start saving on every parcel.**
3. **When a customer emails asking about their delivery, I want to answer in 30 seconds without leaving my inbox, so I stop spending half an hour a day on tracking queries.**

## Conversion triggers (what makes them book a call)
1. Black Friday or a seasonal peak is approaching and last year's manual process nearly broke the operation.
2. A Royal Mail price increase arrives and they search for alternatives for the first time.
3. They see a competitor mention saving money on shipping and wonder what they are missing.

## Disqualifiers (what makes them bounce)
1. Minimum volume commitments. They ship 40 parcels a day and a "500 parcels/month minimum" kills the conversation immediately.
2. A complex onboarding process. If setup takes more than a day, they will not finish it.
3. Pricing that is unclear before sign-up. They are cost-sensitive and will not enter a call to find out the price.
4. Enterprise language. Words like "orchestration," "routing rules," and "SLA compliance" make them feel the product is not for them.

## Words they use vs. words they reject

| Check tracking | Tracking visibility |
| Carrier website | Carrier portal |
| Parcel rate | Rate card |
| Dispatch | Fulfilment |
| Customer email | WISMO ticket |
| Returns | Reverse logistics |
| Cheapest carrier | Rate optimisation |
| Pack and ship | Order processing |
| Price per parcel | Per-unit shipping cost |

## Competitive context
Small business shippers live in Royal Mail Click and Drop, Evri Business Direct, or a basic Shopify Shipping integration. When they hear about multi-carrier platforms, they assume the cost is prohibitive or the setup is too technical. Competitors in consideration include Shippo (US-focused but used), Parcel2Go (consumer-grade), and SendCloud (strong EU positioning). The real competitor is the habit of logging into a carrier portal every morning.

## Primary conversion goal
Run the Domestic Savings Estimator, see a per-parcel saving versus their current carrier, and submit the lead capture form.

## Voice notes for this ICP
- Never use operator vocabulary without explaining it. Write "where's my order emails" not "WISMO queries."
- Time savings land harder than cost savings for this audience. They are time-poor before they are margin-poor.
- No minimum volume messaging should appear prominently. It removes the biggest objection before they raise it.
- Keep sentences short. They are reading on a phone between packing orders.
- The Peak Commerce case study (label generation from 3 hours to 15 minutes daily) is the closest analogue, scaled down.
