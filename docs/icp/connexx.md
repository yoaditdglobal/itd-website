# ICP — Connexx (Product Evaluator)

## The mode
They are 3 to 6 weeks into evaluating multi-carrier shipping platforms for a business that has outgrown its current process. They have a shortlist of 3 or 4 platforms. They have already read the integrations page. They have noted which ERPs and carriers are supported. Now they are trying to determine whether the platform is genuinely built for their operation or whether it is a horizontal product with a thin vertical skin over the top. They will book a demo. But they will arrive at that demo with specific questions about rate engine logic, ERP write-back architecture, and onboarding timeline.

## Who they are
- Job title(s): Head of Operations, CTO, VP of Logistics, IT Manager, Chief Operating Officer, Director of Fulfilment
- Company size: Any size that has outgrown manual carrier management (typically £2m+ revenue)
- Team size: Evaluating on behalf of a 5–50 person ops or tech function
- Tooling: Currently using a mix of carrier portals, a shipping plugin (ShipStation, Shiptheory, or similar), and an ERP or WMS that they need Connexx to integrate with
- Reporting line: Reports to COO or CTO; the buying decision typically requires sign-off from one layer above them

## Top 3 pains (in their words)
1. **"We have read every comparison blog and we still cannot tell which platform actually does what it says it does"** — Carrier coverage lists look identical across competitors. Every platform claims real-time rate comparison. Nobody explains whether that means a live API call on each shipment or a cached rate from the previous day. The evaluator needs to see the architecture, not the marketing.
2. **"We went through a 3-month onboarding with our last platform and it was never fully live"** — A failed or partial platform implementation is the worst case. The evaluator has either lived through it or knows someone who has. They are not buying a promise of integration. They are buying evidence that the integration to their specific ERP version works today.
3. **"We cannot get a straight answer on pricing until we are halfway through a sales process"** — Opaque pricing that requires a sales call to unlock signals that the number is negotiable in ways that favour the vendor. The evaluator wants to see a pricing model they can take to their CFO before the first demo.

## Top 3 jobs-to-be-done
1. **When I am evaluating Connexx against Shiptheory and Metapack, I want to see a live rate comparison on a shipment I define, so I can verify that the rate engine works as described rather than taking the sales team's word for it.**
2. **When I am assessing integration complexity, I want to speak to someone who has connected Connexx to my specific ERP version, so I understand the actual timeline and data mapping requirements before I commit.**
3. **When I am preparing a business case for my CFO, I want accurate per-shipment cost savings based on my actual carrier spend, so I can present a credible ROI rather than a vendor-supplied estimate.**

## Conversion triggers (what makes them book a call)
1. They find a case study that matches their industry, volume, and ERP closely enough to trust the numbers.
2. A competitor's platform fails a technical due diligence question that Connexx answers directly on the integrations page.
3. They run the savings estimator and the output is specific enough (by carrier, by lane, by volume tier) to take to a CFO.

## Disqualifiers (what makes them bounce)
1. The integrations page does not list their ERP or lists it without version specificity. They will not assume compatibility.
2. Pricing is not visible without a sales call. They move to the next platform on the shortlist.
3. The case studies are generic ("a leading eCommerce brand"). No company name, no specific metric, no named contact. Generic proof is no proof.
4. The demo is a pre-recorded walkthrough rather than a live session. They need to ask the hard questions in real time.
5. The sales process is slow. If it takes more than 48 hours to schedule a demo after requesting one, the evaluator concludes the post-sale support will be similar.

## Competitive context
The Connexx evaluator has Shiptheory, Sendcloud, and Metapack on their shortlist alongside ITD. Shiptheory is seen as clean and UK-focused but limited at scale. Sendcloud is strong in EU eCommerce but weaker in B2B and customs. Metapack is the enterprise-grade option, often perceived as over-engineered for businesses under £50m revenue. Connexx's differentiation is specific: case studies with named companies and verifiable metrics, named carrier and ERP integrations, and a rate engine that runs a live API call rather than returning cached rates.

## Primary conversion goal
Book a live 30-minute technical demo scoped to their ERP, their carrier mix, and their specific volume tier. The demo should include a live rate comparison on a real shipment they define.

## Voice notes for this ICP
- This buyer does not need to be sold. They need to be verified. Every claim on the product page should be provable in the first demo.
- Specificity is the differentiator. Named case studies, named ERP versions, named carriers, specific metrics. Generic claims lose this buyer to whoever is more specific.
- Address the onboarding failure fear directly. The SwiftLog case study (client onboarding from 2 weeks to 2 days) reframes the onboarding risk as manageable.
- The CTO variant of this buyer wants API documentation before a sales call. Link to the developer docs early.
- The Head of Ops variant wants to know the post-go-live support model. Who is their account manager, what is the SLA for a carrier connection failure at 8am on a Monday?
