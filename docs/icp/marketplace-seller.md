# ICP — Marketplace Seller

## The mode
They are listing on Amazon, eBay, and at least two other platforms simultaneously. Every morning starts the same way: open five browser tabs, check each marketplace dashboard for overnight orders, manually move them into a dispatch queue, and start selecting carriers. They know exactly what Amazon's late shipment rate threshold is (4%) and exactly how close they are to it at any given time. Penalty fees are not an abstract risk. They have paid them.

## Who they are
- Job title(s): Marketplace Manager, Fulfilment Manager, Founder/Seller, eCommerce Operations Manager, Head of Marketplace
- Company size: £500k–£10m revenue, 200–2,000 orders per day across platforms
- Team size: 2–8 people in dispatch and ops
- Tooling: Amazon Seller Central, eBay Seller Hub, possibly Linnworks or Selro for order aggregation, individual carrier accounts for Royal Mail, Evri, DPD
- Reporting line: Reports to MD or is the MD; key relationships are with marketplace account managers and carrier account reps

## Top 3 pains (in their words)
1. **"Amazon has different label requirements from eBay and both of them changed in the last 12 months"** — Every platform update means a manual audit of label templates, dimensions, and data fields. Miss one change and a batch of shipments gets rejected, triggering a late delivery flag that takes three months of good performance to recover from.
2. **"On a high-volume day we miss orders because there is no single queue"** — When 400 orders come in across five platforms during a promotion, the tab-switching workflow breaks down. An order buried in Etsy gets missed, ships late, and costs a 5-star rating.
3. **"We are paying penalty fees and we cannot always tell which platform or carrier caused them"** — Penalty invoices from Amazon and eBay are itemised by ASIN or listing, not by carrier or SLA breach type. Tracing a penalty back to a dispatch decision takes hours and by then the problem has already recurred.

## Top 3 jobs-to-be-done
1. **When orders arrive across five platforms overnight, I want a single dispatch queue with SLA deadlines and carrier assignments already populated, so I can start printing labels without opening a single marketplace dashboard.**
2. **When Amazon updates its Buy Shipping requirements, I want the label format and carrier eligibility to update automatically, so I do not find out about the change after a compliance rejection.**
3. **When I receive a penalty fee invoice, I want to trace it back to the specific shipment, carrier, and platform in under two minutes, so I can identify the root cause and stop it recurring.**

## Conversion triggers (what makes them book a call)
1. A penalty fee month exceeds £1,000 and the seller decides the tab-switching workflow is no longer acceptable.
2. They are expanding to a new marketplace (TikTok Shop, Zalando, Temu) and know from experience how much manual setup that will require.
3. Their Amazon late shipment rate creeps above 3% and they need a reliable fix before it triggers a suspension review.

## Disqualifiers (what makes them bounce)
1. The platform only connects to Amazon and eBay. If Etsy, TikTok Shop, or Temu are missing, they cannot consolidate their full order flow.
2. SLA-aware carrier selection is not clearly demonstrated. They need to see that the system knows each platform's dispatch deadline and routes accordingly.
3. The pricing model charges per order rather than per parcel. High-volume sellers with low-margin items cannot absorb per-order fees.
4. No mention of seller rating protection. If the product does not address the penalty fee problem, they bounce immediately.

## Competitive context
Most marketplace sellers have tried Linnworks or Selro for order aggregation and use their built-in carrier connections. When those prove insufficient they evaluate Shiptheory (clean, UK-focused, good Amazon integration) and ChannelAdvisor (heavier, analytics-led). The Velocity Sellers case study (penalty fees to zero, fulfilment time from 72 to 24 hours, seller ratings from 94% to 99.2%) is the strongest proof point in the ITD portfolio for this ICP.

## Primary conversion goal
Book a 20-minute platform walkthrough scoped specifically to their marketplace mix. The demo should show the unified order queue and SLA-aware routing in the first five minutes.

## Voice notes for this ICP
- Penalty fees are the emotional centre of this ICP's pain. Name them in the first screen they see.
- Marcus Webb's quote ("Our penalty fees went to zero overnight") is the highest-converting proof point for this audience.
- Use marketplace-specific vocabulary: late shipment rate, Buy Shipping, seller rating, ASIN. It signals the product is built for them, not retrofitted.
- The fear of platform suspension is real and present. Do not be coy about it. Name it.
- Avoid logistics abstraction. "SLA-aware routing" means nothing to them. "The system knows Amazon's cutoff is 4pm and routes accordingly" means everything.
