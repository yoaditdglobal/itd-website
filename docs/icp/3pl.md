# ICP — 3PL

## The mode
They are running fulfilment for 20 to 80 client brands, each with their own carrier preferences, packaging rules, and delivery SLAs. Their biggest operational risk is not a bad quarter. It is a single error batch that causes a client to churn. They are trying to onboard three new clients this quarter without adding headcount, and the manual configuration process for each new brand is eating two weeks of ops time per client.

## Who they are
- Job title(s): Managing Director, Head of Operations, Fulfilment Manager, Commercial Director, Warehouse Manager
- Company size: 500–10,000 parcels per day across all clients
- Team size: 10–60 warehouse and ops staff; 2–5 people managing carrier accounts and client configuration
- Tooling: A WMS (Mintsoft, ShipHero, or proprietary), individual carrier accounts, manual spreadsheets for per-client reporting
- Reporting line: MD reports to board or owner; Ops team reports to MD; client-facing account managers report to Commercial Director

## Top 3 pains (in their words)
1. **"Onboarding a new client takes two weeks and half of that is configuring their carrier preferences and packaging rules"** — Every brand has a different carrier whitelist, different label dimensions, and different routing rules. Setup is done manually in a spreadsheet and then re-entered into the WMS. One misconfiguration causes an incorrect shipment, and the first the ops team knows about it is a client call.
2. **"Our customs error rate on international shipments is costing us client trust, not just money"** — At a 5–7% documentation error rate on cross-border shipments, the question is not whether a client will receive a customs hold. It is when. Each hold prompts an escalation that takes a senior ops manager off the floor for two hours to resolve.
3. **"We cannot give clients a clear view of their own shipping data without exporting a spreadsheet"** — Clients want live visibility into their shipment volume, cost per carrier, and exception rate. Sending a monthly Excel report is not acceptable to brands who have real-time data in every other part of their business.

## Top 3 jobs-to-be-done
1. **When I am onboarding a new client, I want their carrier preferences, packaging rules, and routing logic configured once in a single system, so setup takes two days rather than two weeks.**
2. **When I am dispatching cross-border shipments for a client, I want customs documentation generated automatically from the product data and HS codes already in the system, so errors do not reach the carrier.**
3. **When a client asks for their monthly performance data, I want to pull a branded report in under five minutes, so account management does not require an ops analyst to spend half a day in spreadsheets.**

## Conversion triggers (what makes them book a call)
1. A client threatens to leave after two customs-related delays in the same quarter.
2. They win a new client who ships to 15 countries and realise their current process cannot handle the documentation volume.
3. A competitor 3PL pitches their prospective client with a live Connexx dashboard demo and the prospect asks why they cannot do the same.

## Disqualifiers (what makes them bounce)
1. The platform is built for single-brand shippers. If there is no clear multi-client or child account architecture, the 3PL cannot use it.
2. No WMS integration. If it does not connect to Mintsoft or ShipHero, they have to run two parallel systems and the value disappears.
3. Per-shipment pricing that is not structured for resale. They need to know the cost model before they can build a margin into their client pricing.
4. No compliance engine. A platform that handles routing but not customs documentation solves half the problem and creates a false sense of security.

## Competitive context
3PLs at this scale typically run a WMS (Mintsoft is dominant in UK mid-market 3PLs) with direct carrier connections and manual customs processes handled by a dedicated compliance person. When they evaluate a multi-carrier platform, they look at Metapack (often too heavy and enterprise-priced), Shiptheory (lacks multi-client architecture), and ITD's Connexx. The SwiftLog case study (onboarding from 2 weeks to 2 days, shipment accuracy from 93% to 98.7%, 15 new clients without headcount addition) is a direct-to-ICP proof point.

## Primary conversion goal
Book a 30-minute walkthrough of the multi-client dashboard and the client onboarding workflow. The demo should show child account configuration and the customs compliance engine.

## Voice notes for this ICP
- The 3PL's real product is accuracy and trust. Every conversation should reinforce that Connexx protects both.
- James Thornton's quote ("Our accuracy was our biggest vulnerability. Now it's our strongest selling point.") is the gold-standard opener for this ICP.
- Quantify the onboarding time saving (2 weeks to 2 days) early. It is the most concrete ROI for a 3PL trying to grow without hiring.
- Speak to the commercial value of client retention. A 3PL losing one client to a customs error loses months of revenue.
- Do not use generic "grow your business" language. 3PL MDs hear that constantly. Say "onboard 3 clients a quarter without adding ops headcount."
