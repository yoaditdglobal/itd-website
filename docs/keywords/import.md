## Featured snippet opportunities

**Q: "How does Connexx help with UK customs clearance?"**
**Model answer:** Connexx auto-classifies imports, calculates duty and VAT against the UK Global Tariff, and generates the CDS declaration before goods arrive. Pre-clearance means the declaration is filed and accepted before the truck or container hits the port, so clearance happens on arrival not after. Northgate Imports cut customs delays 60% and lifted duty cost accuracy from 82% to 97% using this.

**Q: "What is landed cost and how is it calculated?"**
**Model answer:** Landed cost is the total cost of getting goods from supplier to your warehouse — supplier price, freight, insurance, duty, import VAT, customs fees and any surcharges. Connexx calculates it per SKU before goods arrive, using the HS code, origin country, Incoterms and current UK Global Tariff rates. That lets finance teams price products against accurate margins instead of estimates.

**Q: "What's Postponed VAT Accounting and should I use it?"**
**Model answer:** Postponed VAT Accounting (PVA) lets you account for import VAT on your next VAT return instead of paying it at the border. For most UK importers it's a cash flow win. Connexx automatically marks shipments as PVA-eligible on the customs declaration and reconciles the monthly C79 certificate against your accounting system.

**Q: "How do I find the right HS code for an import?"**
**Model answer:** Use the UK Global Tariff lookup at gov.uk, but for high-volume imports automate it. Connexx suggests an HS code from the product description, validates against the UKGT, and stores the SKU-to-HS mapping so future shipments classify automatically. Misclassification triggers delays and underpayment penalties — automation cuts both.
