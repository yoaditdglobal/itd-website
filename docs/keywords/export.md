## Featured snippet opportunities

**Q: "What documents do I need to export from the UK to the EU?"**
**Model answer:** Five core documents. A commercial invoice (value, HS code, EORI). A packing list. A customs declaration through CDS (your forwarder or platform handles this). A certificate of origin where preference applies (EUR1 for UK-EU TCA goods). And an Incoterms-aligned contract specifying who pays duty (DDP, DAP). Connexx auto-generates all five from one sales order entry, with country-specific rules for every EU destination.

**Q: "How does Connexx handle export documentation?"**
**Model answer:** Connexx auto-generates commercial invoices, packing lists, certificates of origin and CDS declarations for every export based on HS code, destination country and shipment value. Country-specific rules update automatically — Meridian Trade Co cut documentation time from 4 hours to 1 hour per shipment using this. Customs holds due to paperwork errors dropped 90%.

**Q: "What is an EORI number and how do I get one?"**
**Model answer:** An EORI (Economic Operator Registration and Identification) number is the unique ID HMRC uses to track your exports. UK EORIs start with GB. You apply at gov.uk and receive it in 3 working days. If you ship to the EU you also need an EU EORI, applied for in any EU member state. Connexx stores both EORIs and applies the correct one per shipment automatically.

**Q: "Do I need IOSS if I'm exporting B2C to the EU?"**
**Model answer:** Yes, if the consignment is under €150 and shipped to an EU consumer. IOSS (Import One-Stop Shop) lets you charge EU VAT at checkout and pay it monthly to one tax authority, so the customer doesn't get hit with import VAT on delivery. Connexx applies your IOSS number to every qualifying consignment, generates the right commercial invoice, and routes via IOSS-compatible carriers (DHL Express, DPD, Evri EU).
