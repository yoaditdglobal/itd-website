## Featured snippet opportunities

**Q: "How does Connexx work for B2B wholesale shipping?"**
**Model answer:** Connexx integrates with SAP, NetSuite, Dynamics and Sage to automate wholesale dispatch. When a sales order is confirmed, the rate engine compares parcel carriers (DPD, DHL, Parcelforce, UPS) and pallet networks (Pall-Ex, Palletline, Palletways) by weight, destination and delivery window. The right carrier is booked automatically. Atlas Industrial automated 90% of their wholesale routing this way and cut redelivery costs to near zero.

**Q: "What's the difference between parcel and pallet shipping for B2B?"**
**Model answer:** Parcel covers consignments under ~30kg single-piece, typically through DPD, Parcelforce, DHL or UPS. Pallet covers anything heavier or larger, through pallet networks like Pall-Ex, Palletline, Palletways or DX Freight. Connexx rate-shops across both layers in one workflow, so you pick the cheapest compliant lane for every order without manually deciding parcel vs pallet.

**Q: "Does Connexx integrate with SAP for B2B shipping?"**
**Model answer:** Yes. Connexx integrates with SAP S/4HANA and SAP ECC. Sales orders and deliveries sync into Connexx automatically. The rate engine picks the right carrier per consignment. Tracking numbers, PODs and audit logs write back to SAP for compliance. Atlas Industrial dispatch team handles 3x the volume on the same headcount since the integration went live.

**Q: "How do I book a timed delivery to a UK DC?"**
**Model answer:** Use a carrier that supports timed and booked-in delivery (DPD AM, DHL Time Definite, Parcelforce Express AM, or a pallet network with booking-in service). The booking reference and delivery slot must travel with the manifest. Connexx stores the DC's booking-in profile per customer, generates the BOL with the reference embedded, and routes through the right service automatically.
